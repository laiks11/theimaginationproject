About us

We are defending how the next generation learns to think.

The Imagination Project is a Texas based nonprofit building critical thinking and creative confidence in young people ages 4 to 18, so they can meet a world full of artificial intelligence with judgment, imagination, and agency of their own.

Our mission

To build the thinking that cannot be automated.

We build critical thinking skills in elementary, middle, and high school students through mentorship, real world experience, and credentialed programming, so the next generation can think independently and use technology as a tool rather than a crutch.

Our vision

A generation that thinks for itself.

We imagine a generation of intelligently disobedient young leaders whose capacity to think, reason, and create cannot be automated, and who share that power to build a more just and imaginative world.

Why it matters

The thinking schools were built to develop is quietly being handed away.

Research increasingly links heavy reliance on generative AI with a measurable decline in critical thinking. Nearly every student now uses these tools, often alone and without guidance, while classrooms have not yet caught up. We step into that gap, early and on purpose.

Our values

Four commitments that shape every program.

01

Intellectual courage

We ask students to sit with hard questions longer than is comfortable, and to enjoy the work of figuring them out.

02

Human agency

Our programs are designed so that the student's own responsibility, choices, and voice always come first.

03

Equity

Free multilingual resources and scholarship seats keep the door open wide, because talent is everywhere.

04

Cultural curiosity

Our work is cross national, multilingual, and interdisciplinary, so students learn well beyond a single point of view.

Our founder
Every future leader is young today, and needs room to think and to build something real. TIP teaches young people to think independently, draw from real-world sources rather than summaries, and bring to fruition their vision of the world.

Laika Kalyani Patel founded The Imagination Project in 2025, the same month she was crowned Miss Texas Teen USA. Within four months she had written and delivered the Dear Future Leader curriculum to more than five hundred elementary students across Texas, and the accompanying children's book now funds literacy and hygiene programs for over eight hundred women served by Manav Sadhna in Ahmedabad, India.

She serves as President of Texas DECA and brings experience across competitive business, cultural exchange, service, and the arts. That range is the model at the heart of the organization: global impact through local action, and whole person capability in every young person we reach.

Laika Kalyani PatelFounder & Founder, The Imagination Project
