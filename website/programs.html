Our programs

A developmental sequence, from your first challenge to a seat on a council.

Every program is anchored in one conviction: the capacity to think independently, apply reason, and lead with imagination has to be actively cultivated. Here is the whole portfolio, grouped by where it stands today.

Now / Available today

Programs you can join this month.

Community offerings built to reach students regardless of geography, income, or access to technology.

Now

ThinkFirst Mentorship Circle

Weekly · 3 month cohort

Small groups of four to six students meet for forty five minutes to explore a subject they helped choose. Using a syntopical reading approach across several books and articles, they practice comparing, questioning, and synthesizing, building toward a capstone project of their own design. Mentors are high school volunteers who guide the discussion.

Ages 5 to 18Active
Now

Core Skills Lab

Monthly · Free

Each month targets one cognitive skill, from logical reasoning and source evaluation to ethical judgment and data literacy. Every challenge comes with a grade level reading and a word bank, and is fully completable with paper and pen. Available in English, Hindi, Spanish, and French, with more languages as the community grows.

Open to allActive
Now

The Socratic Circle

Monthly · 90 minutes

Live workshops bring working professionals into dialogue with students, each showing how creativity and critical thinking operate in their specific field. Sessions follow our AI for Good framework and are recorded and archived as free content, with a new speaker every month.

Live, onlineActive

Programs on the near horizon.

Credentialed depth and real audiences, rolling out across our first national phase.

Next

ImaginationU Certificate Track

Flagship · 3 month, one to one

Our signature program pairs each student with a mentor whose expertise matches their interest, across three progressive pillars: think, create, and lead. Graduates receive a certificate with a unique, verifiable credential ID, with shareable digital badges planned so achievements can travel to college applications and beyond.

Year 1 to 2Coming soon
Next

Future Leaders Summit

Annual · One day

A yearly summit convening leaders across medicine, technology, the humanities, policy, and the arts, speaking directly to young people. Students submit the questions that structure each interview. Scholarship seats bring students from under resourced communities in person, and the livestream is free worldwide.

Year 2Coming soon
Next

Young Minds World Fair

Annual · Multi track

Our annual showcase, where students present original projects to practitioners across tracks like AI and society, health and medicine, environment and climate, and the humanities. Every participant receives written feedback; track winners earn a cash prize and one to one tutelage with a senior professional.

Year 3 to 4Coming soon

Where we are headed.

An international structure that gives young people real power, not symbolic recognition.

Future

Global Pairs

6 month collaboration

Part of our Global Imagination Network, Global Pairs connects students across countries, beginning with India and the United States, to build a shared project together. Winning pairs earn a real shared experience, from a co published piece to an in person meetup, expanding as the network grows.

Year 5 and beyondPlanned
Future

Youth Innovation Council

Selective · Advisory

Our most selective program. Students who have shown real engagement apply through examination and interview, then take seats on advisory boards inside companies, schools, clinics, libraries, and within The Imagination Project itself, bringing the perspective of the incoming generation to decisions that shape it.
