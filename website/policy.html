The Policy Lab

A child's capacity to think is worth protecting in law.

The Policy Lab is our long term commitment to advocacy. We start close to home and move outward, working toward a future in which every student's attention and independent reasoning are treated as something worth defending.

The manifesto

Why we are doing this.

The window between AI's rapid arrival in classrooms and the support students need to use it well is closing. We intend to keep it open.

A growing body of research shows that frequent reliance on generative AI correlates with a measurable decline in critical thinking. As students hand the hard work of reasoning to a machine, the very habits that schools exist to build begin to atrophy. Nearly every student now uses these tools, often alone and without guidance, while curricula have not yet caught up.

We hold a simple position. The capacity to reason, evaluate evidence, and impose meaning is the defining feature of a fully realized human life. Its erosion is not only an educational problem. It is a civic one, and it falls hardest on the communities with the least support. Policy should treat it that way.

Our positions

What we are advocating for.

Two near term asks that are clear, achievable, and overdue, and one principle that guides everything beyond them.

01

AI disclosure in classrooms

Students and families deserve to know when and how AI is used in learning and assessment. Honest disclosure is the floor, not the ceiling, of a healthy relationship with these tools.

02

A minimum of one day

Every student should receive at least one full day of genuine AI education each year: how these systems work, where they fail, and how to think clearly alongside them rather than defer to them.

03

Protect cognitive agency

Public funding and policy should recognize student attention and independent reasoning as a shared good worth defending, and measure programs by whether they build it.

04

Equity at the center

The communities most exposed to AI driven disruption are the least equipped to navigate it. Every policy we pursue is designed to narrow that divide, not widen it.

The roadmap

From Texas, outward.

We believe durable policy is built the way durable thinking is: patiently, with evidence, and in good company.

NowTexas

Build the case at home.

We gather evidence from our own programs and partners, and bring AI disclosure and a one day education standard to our home state, where our community and pilot cohorts already live.

NextAcross states

Carry the model outward.

We translate what works into model language for neighboring states, and build a coalition of educators, families, researchers, and institutional partners who can advance it together.

ThenNational

Shape the national conversation.

We elevate student cognitive agency within federal funding and public discourse, building on real bipartisan momentum around AI literacy in K to 12 education.

BeyondThe horizon

Protect cognition for everyone.

In time, our advocacy broadens beyond youth, toward protecting human cognition across generations, including learners with special needs and those facing neurodegenerative conditions.
